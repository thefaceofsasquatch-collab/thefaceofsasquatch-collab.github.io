Dashboard — Help

A guide to reading the SEI Intelligence Dashboard — including all the interactive elements that aren't obviously clickable.

What the Dashboard Is

The Dashboard is a high-level intelligence summary of every Bigfoot encounter video SEI has analyzed. It shows aggregate statistics — tone distributions, behavior frequencies, regional patterns, subject descriptions — drawn from the full encounter database.

The Dashboard is read-only: it shows summaries, not individual encounters. To search or filter individual encounters, use the Encounter Browser. To see what was added in the most recent intake run, use Latest Update.

Key insight: Many elements on the Dashboard are clickable and will take you directly to a pre-filtered view in the Encounter Browser. These links are not visually highlighted — look for the dotted underline on bar chart labels, or simply try clicking any number, tone box, or behavior label.

Reading the Charts

Tone Distribution Boxes

Four boxes showing the count and percentage of encounter accounts classified by emotional tone:

  • Negative — frightening, threatening, or distressing encounters.
  • Mixed — encounters with both fear and curiosity, awe, or positive feeling.
  • Neutral — matter-of-fact observations with no strong emotional response.
  • Positive — peaceful, curious, or uplifting encounters.

The stacked bar chart to the right shows the same data as a proportional visual. Click any tone box to see those encounters in the Encounter Browser.

Behavior and Strangeness Bar Charts

Horizontal bar charts ranked by frequency. The bar length is proportional to the count relative to the most common item. The number on the right is the raw count of encounters where that behavior or claim was observed.

Behaviors are standardized tags describing specific actions (e.g., Vocalizations, Wood Knocking, Paralleling / Walking Alongside). High Strangeness items are unusual or paranormal claims reported by witnesses (e.g., Cloaking / Invisibility, Telepathy / Mind Speak).

Sightings by Region

Geographic regions where encounters took place, ranked by count. Each bar uses a distinct color for readability. Click any region label to see all encounters from that region in the Encounter Browser.

Subject Age Distribution

A stacked bar showing the proportion of described subjects classified as Adult, Juvenile, or Unknown age. This is based on witness descriptions extracted from video transcripts — many encounters do not include age information, so Unknown is typically the largest segment.

Hair / Coat Color

Colored chips showing the distribution of reported hair or coat colors across all described subjects. Colors are extracted directly from witness descriptions.

From the Latest Update — Encounter Cards

This section shows a rich card for each encounter logged in the most recent weekly intake run. Each card includes:

  • Video title — links to YouTube.
  • Channel name, date, and region — metadata for the encounter.
  • Tone badge — color-coded emotional tone of the account.
  • AI-generated description — a brief summary of the encounter in italics.
  • Behavior chips — Vocalizations Wood Knocking — tags for observed behaviors.
  • High Strangeness chips — Cloaking / Invisibility — if any unusual claims were reported.
  • Deep Link — a timestamped YouTube link jumping to the exact moment of the encounter in the video. Labeled "► Deep Link @ M:SS →".

If a video contains multiple sighting accounts, each account appears as a separate card with its own deep link.

Account of the Month

The featured encounter at the bottom of the Dashboard is selected automatically by composite score: a formula that weights sighting count, behavior count, and high strangeness count to identify the most data-rich encounter of the period.

Two links appear on the card:

  • ▶ Watch the full account → — opens the YouTube video.
  • 🔍 Browse all [Channel] encounters → — opens the Encounter Browser pre-filtered to all encounters from that channel, so you can explore the full body of work.

Navigating the Platform

The Dashboard connects to three other pages via the Quick Nav Bar and the call-to-action section at the bottom:

PageWhat it's for
🔍 Encounter Browser Search and filter every individual encounter in the database. Sort by date, channel, tone, or region. Use behavior and strangeness filters. Share filtered URLs.
▶ Latest Update A focused view of the most recent intake run — what was added, which channels were processed, and a summary of new encounters.
🎲 Discover A curated browse experience — encounter cards with deep links organized by behavior, designed for exploration rather than search.
