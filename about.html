About This App | A note to content creators

As someone who detests people stealing my imagery, the last thing I want to do is steal anyone else's IP. So I want to be straight with you about what this tool actually does.

It doesn't share your content. It directs people to it.

Here's what it actually does:

  • Links go directly to the original YouTube source
  • The only thing it displays is thumbnails, the same way any link on any platform does
  • Your channel is credited by name throughout
  • It's essentially a deeper search than YouTube provides, using data you've already chosen to share publicly
  • Filters include encounter tone: negative, mixed, neutral, or positive (benevolent interactions would be positive) and behaviors like rock throwing, vocalizations, portals, and others

Think of it like a movie review that doesn't give away spoilers. It points people toward content they'll want to find, without reproducing anything.

The goal is to be something enthusiasts and/or researchers find useful for discovering content they like and getting sent directly to creators like you.

No data is being taken. Just indexed and pointed back to the source.

If you have any concerns about how your channel appears, reach out. My intention is to be helpful, not harmful.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does SEI get its data?
We use an automated pipeline to monitor specific YouTube channels. When a new video is published, we analyze the public transcript using AI to identify if it contains a first-hand encounter account. If it does, we extract the behaviors, tone, region, and exact timestamps, and add them to our database.
What is a "canonical behavior tag"?
Witnesses describe behaviors in many different ways (e.g., "knocking on wood", "tree knocks", "banging on a trunk"). We map all of these to a single canonical tag — in this case, Wood Knocking. This allows you to search across all channels and find every instance of a behavior, regardless of the exact words used.
Why do some videos have multiple accounts?
Many channels feature call-in shows or compilation episodes where several different witnesses share their stories in a single video. SEI treats each distinct story as a separate "sighting account" with its own row in the database and its own deep link.
How often is the database updated?
We run a weekly intake process that checks our monitored channels for new uploads and processes them automatically. Check the "Latest Update" page to see what was added in the most recent run.
Can I suggest a channel to monitor?
We are currently in a closed pre-beta phase and monitoring a specific set of channels to refine our taxonomy. A channel nomination form will be available in the future.
