Encounter Browser — Help

A complete guide to searching, filtering, and navigating the SEI Encounter Browser.

Overview

The Encounter Browser is a searchable, filterable database of every Bigfoot encounter video analyzed by SEI. Each row in the database represents a single sighting account — one reported encounter within a video. A single video may contain multiple accounts, each with its own row.

The browser has three views, selectable from the tab bar at the top of the page:

Sightings Database
Channel Profiles
Channel Comparison

View 1 — Sightings Database

The default view. Shows every analyzed encounter as a table row (desktop) or card (mobile), with full filter and sort controls.

Filters

Use any combination of the controls in the filter bar to narrow results. All filters work together — results must match all active filters simultaneously.

Filter What it does URL Param
⌕ Search box Free-text search across behaviors, region, channel name, and encounter notes. Partial matches work — typing "wood" will match "Wood Knocking". ?kw=wood+knocking
Channel Filter to a single YouTube channel. Shows only encounters from that channel's videos. ?channel=Sasquatch+Chronicles
Tone Filter by the overall emotional tone of the encounter account — Negative, Mixed, Neutral, or Positive. ?tone=Negative
Region Filter by the geographic region where the encounter took place (e.g., Pacific Northwest, Southeast, Great Lakes). ?region=Pacific+Northwest
Month Filter to encounters from videos published in a specific month (YYYY-MM format). ?month=2026-04
Strangeness Show only encounters that include High Strangeness claims (e.g., cloaking, telepathy, orbs), or only those that do not. ?strange=yes
☰ Behaviors Multi-select behavior picker. Choose one or more canonical behavior tags — results must include at least one of the selected behaviors (OR logic). A badge shows how many behaviors are active. ?behaviors=Vocalizations|Wood+Knocking
Tip: Clicking a behavior chip, strangeness chip, or tone pill anywhere in the results automatically applies that tag as a filter — no need to type it manually.

Sorting

Click any column header to sort by that column. Click again to reverse the sort direction. An arrow indicator (▲ / ▼) shows the active sort. Sortable columns: Date, Channel, Tone, Region.

Pagination

Results are shown 50 per page. Use the ‹ Prev and Next › buttons or the numbered page buttons to navigate. The counter at the right shows your current range (e.g., "1–50 of 143").

Result Count

The badge next to the filter bar always shows how many encounters match your current filters. When no filters are active, it shows the total database size.

Share Results

The 🔗 Share Results button copies a shareable URL to your clipboard. The URL encodes all active filters as query parameters, so anyone who opens it will see the same filtered view. This works for all filter types including multi-select behaviors.

https://thefaceofsasquatch-collab.github.io/browser.html?tone=Negative&region=Pacific+Northwest&behaviors=Vocalizations|Wood+Knocking

Reading an Encounter Row

Each row in the table (or card on mobile) contains:

  • Date — approximate publication date of the source video.
  • Channel — the YouTube channel that published the video.
  • Account / Video — the video title (links to YouTube), a brief AI-generated description of the encounter, subject description if available, and a deep link (see below).
  • Tone — the emotional tone of the account. Click the pill to filter all encounters by that tone.
    Negative Mixed Neutral Positive
  • Region — geographic region of the encounter.
  • Behaviors — canonical behavior tags identified in the account. Click any chip to filter all encounters by that behavior.
    VocalizationsWood KnockingParalleling / Walking Alongside
  • High Strangeness — unusual or paranormal claims reported in the account (if any). Click any chip to filter.
    Cloaking / InvisibilityTelepathy / Mind Speak

Deep Links

Where a precise timestamp has been identified for a behavior in the video, a Deep Link appears below the video title. Clicking it opens YouTube at the exact moment the behavior occurs — no scrubbing required.

If a video contains multiple sighting accounts, each account shows its own deep link labeled Account 1, Account 2, etc., with its individual timestamp.

Clear Filters

The Clear button resets all filters and the search box at once, returning to the full unfiltered database.

View 2 — Channel Profiles

A card grid showing a summary profile for every monitored YouTube channel. Use the search box at the top to filter channels by name.

Each profile card shows:

  • Video count and encounter count — total videos analyzed and sighting accounts found.
  • Sighting rate — percentage of analyzed videos that contained at least one encounter account. Color-coded: green (≥ 50%), yellow (≥ 20%), red (below 20%).
  • Top Behaviors — the most frequently observed behavior tags across all of the channel's encounters, with bar charts. Click any bar to jump to the Sightings Database filtered to that channel and behavior.
  • Top Regions — the most common geographic regions for that channel's encounters. Click any bar to filter.
Tip: Clicking any bar in a Channel Profile card automatically switches to the Sightings Database view, pre-filtered to that channel and the selected tag.

View 3 — Channel Comparison

Side-by-side comparison of any two monitored channels. Select Channel A and Channel B from the dropdowns to populate the comparison cards.

Each comparison card shows:

  • Encounter Tone breakdown — bar chart of Negative / Mixed / Neutral / Positive counts and percentages.
  • Top Behaviors — the 8 most common behavior tags with relative frequency bars.
  • High Strangeness — the 6 most common strangeness claims.
  • Top Regions — the 5 most common geographic regions.
  • Evidence Types — the 5 most common forms of physical or circumstantial evidence mentioned.

Click any bar in the comparison cards to drill into the Sightings Database filtered to that channel and tag.

Deep Linking via URL Parameters

The Encounter Browser supports full deep linking. Any combination of filters can be encoded in the URL and shared. When the page loads with URL parameters, the filters are applied automatically and the Sightings Database view is shown.

Parameter Example value Notes
?kw= wood knocking Free-text keyword search
?channel= Sasquatch Chronicles Must match channel name exactly (case-insensitive)
?tone= Negative Negative, Mixed, Neutral, or Positive
?region= Pacific Northwest Must match a region name in the dropdown
?month= 2026-04 YYYY-MM format
?strange= yes yes or no
?behaviors= Vocalizations|Wood Knocking Pipe-separated list of canonical behavior tag names (OR logic)

Use the 🔗 Share Results button to generate these URLs automatically — no need to construct them by hand.

Glossary

Encounter / Sighting Account

A single first-person or witnessed report of a Bigfoot encounter within a video. One video may contain multiple accounts (e.g., a call-in show with several callers). Each account is analyzed and stored separately.

Tone

The overall emotional character of the encounter as reported by the witness:

  • Negative — frightening, threatening, or distressing encounter.
  • Mixed — elements of both fear and curiosity, awe, or positive feeling.
  • Neutral — matter-of-fact observation with no strong emotional response.
  • Positive — peaceful, curious, or uplifting encounter.

Behaviors

Canonical tags describing specific actions or activities observed during the encounter — for example: Vocalizations Wood Knocking Rock Throwing Paralleling / Walking Alongside. Tags are standardized across all videos so that cross-channel comparisons are meaningful.

High Strangeness

Claims that go beyond a straightforward physical sighting — for example: Cloaking / Invisibility Telepathy / Mind Speak Orbs / Light Phenomena. These are recorded as reported by the witness without editorial judgment.

Deep Link

A YouTube URL with a ?t= timestamp parameter that jumps directly to the moment a specific behavior or encounter occurs in the video. Generated from AI analysis of the video transcript.

Sighting Rate

The percentage of a channel's analyzed videos that contained at least one confirmed encounter account. A high sighting rate indicates a channel that consistently features first-hand reports.

Tips & Tricks

  • Click any chip or pill in the results to instantly filter by that tag — behavior chips, strangeness chips, and tone pills are all clickable.
  • Combine filters freely — for example, filter to Pacific Northwest + Negative tone + Vocalizations behavior to find the most intense reported encounters in that region.
  • Use the Behaviors picker to select multiple behaviors at once. The OR logic means you'll see encounters that have any of the selected behaviors — useful for exploring related tags together.
  • Share Results encodes your entire filter state into a URL. Use it to bookmark a search or share a specific filtered view with others.
  • Channel Comparison is useful for spotting differences in tone, regional focus, and behavior frequency between channels — for example, comparing a wilderness encounter channel against a habituation-focused one.
  • Deep links save significant time when researching a specific behavior — click to jump straight to the relevant moment in the video rather than watching the whole thing.
